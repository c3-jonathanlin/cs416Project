COVID-19 and Politics

COVID-19 Vaccination in CA

Vaccination rates against COVID-19 in California vary from county to county. In this narrative, we examine how vaccination rates correlate to the political leanings of each county.

Below is a histogram showing distribution of the percentage of the population in each county which has received at least one dose of the COVID-19 vaccine, as of July 1, 2021.

We see that most counties had between 30% and 70% of the population having received at least one dose.

Vaccination data obtained from (CA Department of Health and Human Services).

April 2021 Vaccination Rate vs. 2020 Presidential Election Margin

Below we see that there is a relation between the vote margin between the Biden/Trump in the 2020 presidential election and the vaccination rate as of April 2021.

The x-axis represents Donald Trump's vote share minus Joe Biden's vote share. The y-axis represents the proportion of the county having received at least one dose of the vaccine.

There are a number of counties one might consider outliers at this point in time. Mouse over each data point to see the name of the county it represents.

July 2021 Vaccination Rate vs. 2020 Presidential Election Margin

When we use vaccination rates three months later, in July 2021, we see that the relation becomes even clearer and the correlation even higher.

At this point vaccination rate is very predictable to a high degree based on vote margin alone.
